## E. List of challenge KPIs and how the project addressed them

** a) Establish grassroots foothold across 16 different colleges/universities globally** 

The project successfully established presence across **8+ tertiary institutions** through formal partnerships and student enrollment. While the full 16-university target is ongoing, the project built a replicable model for institutional engagement including MOUs with European Business Institute (Luxembourg) and Richfield (South Africa), and formal Work Integrated Learning approvals at Tshwane University of Technology. 


** b) Introduce Cardano technologies to IT tertiary students** 

Developed and delivered comprehensive training materials on Haskell/Plutus, CIP-1694, Midnight, and Hydra L2, all published openly at https://github.com/wimsio/universities/wiki and https://coxygen.co/universities/interactive-email-tutorials. Live facilitation sessions ran for over 7 months, far exceeding the typical 1-day workshop model. 

**c) Upskill students on Cardano Smart Contract Development** 

Scaled from 28 students in February 2025 to **330 enrolled students** by August 2025. Students minted over **2,800 on-chain progress tokens** on Cardano Preprod, providing verifiable proof of learning. The program evolved from self-paced learning to a structured model with **20 facilitators across 3 time zones**.

## G. Key achievements (in particular around collaboration and engagement)

### Strategic Academic Partnerships

The project secured formal collaborations that go beyond typical outreach:

- **European Business Institute (Luxembourg)** – Signed MOU and partnered to offer the **CP108 Haskell Plutus Certification**. Coxygen Global sponsored **47 students** for this program, adding formal academic accreditation to the technical training. Coxygen is listed on the [EBU partners page](https://connect.ebulux.lu/).

- **Richfield Graduate Institute of Technology (South Africa)** – Formal MOU established for student participation.

- **Tshwane University of Technology** – Formal approval for students to complete their Work Integrated Learning (WIL)/Industry Experience capstone projects through Cardano Haskell Plutus development.

- **We Think Code, Inkuba Academy, Ndvedaz Crypto Hub Academy, Victory Youth Group** – CEO/Management level approvals granted for student participation.

### Cross-Project Ecosystem Collaboration

The project gained recognition from other Catalyst initiatives. As noted in the Cardano Forum, Cardano2VN acknowledged: *"Coxygen Global has now 630 enrollment, has partnered with European Business Institute of Luxemburg in certifying Plutus Students. We need global collaboration."* 

### On-Chain Credential Innovation

A key technical achievement was the implementation of **progress token NFTs** with 721 metadata containing evidence of student work. This created a transparent, immutable record of learning that is publicly verifiable on the Cardano Preprod network. The project minted over **2,800 such credentials**, demonstrating a scalable model for educational verification.

### Facilitator Network Development

The project successfully built and managed a team of **10 facilitators** across **3 time zones**, enabling live support and structured learning that addressed the steep Haskell learning curve—a critical evolution from the initial self-paced model that proved ineffective.

### Marketing and Brand Presence

Developed professional marketing materials including branded merchandise (caps, t-shirts) used to build community identity and recognition across campuses.


## H. Key learnings

### Institutional Engagement Requires Patience

Government tertiary institutions have lengthy protocol processes that can delay formal partnerships by months or years. The project learned to balance expectations and maintain engagement while pursuing parallel recruitment through direct student outreach. Private institutions (like EBU and Richfield) proved quicker to formalize due to easier access to CEOs and decision-makers.

### Self-Paced Learning Fails for Haskell

The steep learning curve of Haskell/Plutus made self-paced study ineffective. The project pivoted to **facilitator-led live sessions** with incentivized facilitators, which dramatically improved retention and progress. This required building a team structure and compensating facilitators monthly.

### Wallet Onboarding is a Major Barrier

Students new to Web3 struggled significantly with Cardano wallets. The project responded by developing **Coxy Wallet**—a cross-platform wallet pegged to Preprod with pre-funded addresses—to lower the entry barrier.

### Hardware Limitations Require Creative Solutions

Plutus development typically requires significant RAM, hard drive space, and bandwidth. The project addressed this by:
- Creating PDF worksheets for offline study
- Planning a web-based Haskell Plutus playground with 20+ compiling templates
- Exploring lighter-weight alternatives to full local development environments

### Student Discipline Varies

Remote learning requires self-discipline that many students lack initially. The facilitator model, regular check-ins, and the incentive of EBU certification helped improve consistency. Attendance data shows approximately **5 sessions per active student** on average.

### Marketing Channels Evolved

Institutional marketing departments proved ineffective at recruiting students. Direct advertising and **word-of-mouth** among students became the most effective recruitment channels.

## J. Final thoughts/comments

This project represents a **qualitative shift** in how Cardano conducts university outreach. By moving beyond 1-3 day hackathons to sustained, multi-month engagement with live facilitation, we have demonstrated a replicable model for building genuine developer capacity.

The numbers tell a compelling story: from **28 students in February to 330 by August**, from zero on-chain credentials to **over 2,800 minted**, from a handful of contacts to formal MOUs with respected institutions. But the qualitative impact is equally significant—students are not just learning about Cardano; they are building real skills, earning accredited certifications, and contributing code to public repositories.

The challenges we faced—institutional inertia, the Haskell learning curve, wallet complexity, hardware limitations—are the same challenges any global education initiative must overcome. Our solutions (facilitator networks, custom wallets, offline materials) provide a playbook for future projects.

Most importantly, this project has shown that **Cardano can be taught effectively at scale** to students with no prior blockchain experience. The grassroots foothold we have established across multiple countries and institutions is not just a metric—it is the foundation for the next generation of Cardano developers.

We extend our deepest gratitude to the Cardano community and Catalyst for making this work possible. The investment in education is the investment in Cardano's future.
